Exploratory Analysis

This sections shows some attributes of persons who have taken loans with us. The data used in this analysis is from October- December 2017.

    The following category names are used to represent different categories of users.
  • Complete: -> Customers who completed loans on time
  • Late: -> Customers who completed loans on late
  • Default: -> Customers who defaulted on their loans

Preliminary Analysis

In this section, we are going to carry out some hypothesis testing to test whether loan repayment is affected by some variables or occurs by chance.

Hypothesis #1: Average M-Pesa Transaction Amount

H0:
There is no difference in the average daily mpesa transaction amount between those who complete loans on time, those who are late and those who default.

H1:
There is significant difference in the average daily mpesa transaction amount between those who complete loans on time, those who are late and those who default.

Variables:
  1. average daily mpesa transaction amount (Continuous)
  2. Loan Status(Categorical - completed_on_time, completed_late,default)

Conclusion

There is a difference between the means of the three groups(p=2.86e-06) but that difference is only significant between those who defaulted and those who completed the loans on time. There is no difference in means of daily mpesa transactions for those who completed loans late with any other group. The overall effect size is small.

Hypothesis #4: Number of Dependants

H0:
There is no difference in the average number of dependants between those who complete loans on time, those who are late and those who default.

H1:
There is significant difference in the average number of dependants between those who complete loans on time, those who are late and those who default.

Variables:
  1. average number of dependants (discrete)
  2. Loan Status(Categorical - completed_on_time, completed_late,default)


Test:
Chi Square test of Independence.
Results:

Chi Squared Results

Cramer's V

Conclusion

84% of persons who self-reported as having more than 5 dependants completed the loans on time compared to 62% who reported having no dependants. The p-value of the chi-square test shows some statistical significance, albeit small. The effect is also fairly small, meaning that the predictor is weak (at the moment).

Hypothesis #5: Marital Status

H0 null:
There is no correlation between marital status and loan status.

H1 alternative:
Marital Status is correlated with loan status i.e. whether someone is able to complete loans on time,whether they are late or are in default.

Variables:
  1. Marital status (binary discrete)
  2. Loan Status(Categorical - completed_on_time, completed_late,default)


Test:
Chi Square of Independence.
Results:

Chi Squared Results

Cramer's V

Conclusion

Persons who self report as married, are statistically more likely to repay loans on time compared to their unmarried counterparts. The effect size of this association is however weak, at only 0.1025. This is likely to be a fairly weak predictor.

Machine Learning Model

The first credit-scoring algorithm to be employed will be an XGBoost Model. XGBoost is a robust algorithm of combining weak classifiers into stronger ones.

To Learn more about the XGBoost Algorithm please see this link.

Loanbee's implementation of XGBoost using sklearn is available here!

In summary, the model has an accuracy of 68.81% in distinguishing between complete, late and default categories

Additionally, if we assume we don't consider complete and late as separate categories, we can combine them to one category (non-defaulters). The model has an accuracy of 78.29% in distinguishing between defaulters and non-defaulters
